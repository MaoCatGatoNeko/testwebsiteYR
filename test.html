Objectives of The Lesbian History Project:

to promote lesbian history and visibility.

to create an online record of lesbian history that contains:
information on lesbian accomplishments and contributions.
information on work done and/or in progress;
other related information.

to assist lesbian archives and collections in putting lists of their holdings up on the web.

to assist projects that record, transcribe and preserve oral histories of lesbians, especially lesbians of color (in the u.s. and elsewhere)

to help researchers locate primary witnesses and documents.

to promote ongoing dialogue between academic lgbt scholars and lgbt community scholars and between academics and the lgbt communities in general.

to articulate and promote the importance of native research to lesbian (and gay/bi/tg) history.

to encourage organizations to raise funds and/or set aside funds to support work in lesbian (and gay/bi/tg) history.

to encourage individuals and groups to donate original papers, documents and memorabilia to archives that preserve and promote lesbian history and visibility.

to use the web site as an educational and training tool for women of all ages and backgrounds.

to host an annual meeting for purposes of "support and report" for those doing work in lgbt history, preservation, cataloging, indexing, research and www dissemination.

to network with other projects, collections, archives and associations doing similar or related work.

Ways to Participate in this Project

* Send us articles you have written on this subject along with your permission to post them on the web site.

* Send us bibliographies and chronologies related to lesbian history.

* Send us information about work in progress, unusual bits of lesbian herstory (please cite sources), oral history transcripts and tapes.

* Agree to look over material and information in a specific section of the site and help us with corrections, additions, etc.

* In short we need people to do any/all of the following: gather/locate and send information, enter data, check for errors and omissions, publicize the site, etc. You can do this once or on an ongoing basis, and some of it can be done long-distance.

* You can send information via e-mail. If you send information on a disk: we prefer that it be on a Mac-formatted disk using Word, but Word Perfect for pc or Windows is acceptable and we do accept photocopies if e-mail and/or disk are not possible.

* If you have information related to lesbian history that you want up on the Internet, and you don't know how to code the information in html, we can do that for you, if you send the information via e-mail or disk. We can then link it to your site or give you space on our site.
