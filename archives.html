* Gay and Lesbian Archives of the Pacific Northwest (GLAPN) Web Site
Partial list of lesbian and lesbian-related holdings:
Sarah Cook oral history collection: forty-four cassette recordings done in 1977 and 1978,
also interviews with lesbian and feminist activists in Oregon.
Records of the Campaign for a Hate Free Oregon (No on 9), the state-wide organization that helped to defeat Measure 9, anti-gay initiative in 1992. approx. 13 linear feet of financial records, internal memos, legal papers.
Records of Dignity/Portland (Gay and Lesbian Catholic organization) 1979-1995. approx. 4 linear feet
Records of ACT-UP of the Columbia/Willamette Valley, 1990-1992, and the Radical Activists' Truth Squad (R.A.T.S.) including press clippings, correspondence, t-shirts, Oregon Citizens Alliance broadsheets (1992), posters from ACT-UP demonstration during Pres. George Bush's visit to Portland in 1990.
Swerdlow collection: approx thirty reel to reel tape recordings of the KBOO radio broadcasts of "Homophile Half Hour" 1971-73, interviews with local gay activists, politicians, and community leaders. This talk radio program was one of the earliest "gay" radio programs in the country.
Troen collection: three boxes (approx. 3 linear ft.) of gay liberation periodicals, ephemera, correspondence collected by Roger Troen of the Portland Gay Liberation Communication Center between 1971 and 1979.
We are presently beginning an oral history project that will add considerably more material on the lesbian community in the future.

2. HISTORY PROJECTS

* Austin Lesbian Activists of the 70's Herstory Project
Contains a lot of material on this topic including a timeline and questionnaire. Useful model for other cities wanting to record their lesbian herstory. web site

* The Shango Project:
National Archives for Black Lesbians and Gay Men
P.O. Box 2341 Bloomington, Indiana 47402-2341
The Shango Project is a newly conceived effort to collect, preserve and maintain materials of historical interest which document aspects of African-American Lesbian and Gay existence in contemporary and historical society.

* Public Faces/Private Lives/Boston "collects information related to the LGBT history of Boston, which is included as part of a permanent archives organized by Our Boston Heritage." Useful for other cities wanting to record their lgbt history. web site
