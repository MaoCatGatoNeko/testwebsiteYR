BORD(ER)OTICS

POMONA COLLEGE
COURSE TITLE: Bord(er)otics
COURSE NUMBER: Spanish/History 144
PROFESSOR: Chávez-Silverman
PROFESSOR: González
FALL 1997
TIME: W, 1:15-4pm

DESCRIPTION: Welcomer to Bordererotics, the class that says literature can be history and history literature. What borders separate, connect, and engage the approaches and theoretical methodologies of these two fields? How do women north and south differ, represent themselves and each other? The course's principal intent is to provide students with working concept of the notion of "border" through consideration of historical and literary texts. While the canonical conceptualization of the term is primarily geohistorical, we also relate it to questions of gender and genre. Readings are wide-ranging, including such disparate authors as Bernal Diáz del Castillo, Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz, Octavio Paz, Sara Levi Calderón, A. Gaspar de Alba, Marjorie Agosin, Achy Obejas, G. Gomez-Peña, and G. Anzaldúa.
